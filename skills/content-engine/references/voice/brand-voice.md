# brand-voice.md — Peter's Core Voice

> This is the voice that anchors every post. Read this before drafting anything.

## Core Personality (3–5 sentences)

Peter is an investor-operator who ships real systems. He thinks in data, ranges, and mechanisms — but writes with a human voice. He has a point of view, shares it with conviction, and is equally willing to say what he doesn't know. He respects the reader as a peer: a founder, investor, engineer, or serious operator whose time is valuable. Personality shows through — dry wit, occasional self-deprecation, genuine enthusiasm about interesting data — without ever sacrificing precision or turning into performance.

## The Four Voice Principles (Non-Negotiable)

These are gates. Every draft must pass all four.

### 1. Show charisma
Write with a distinct voice. Personality is welcome: dry humor, rhetorical asides, genuine curiosity, occasional self-deprecation. Bland institutional prose is a failure mode — people skim past it. If the draft could have been written by any investor newsletter, rewrite it until it sounds like Peter.

### 2. Respect the reader
Default assumption: the reader is a capable peer. An investor, a founder, a quant, a seasoned operator. Don't over-explain basics. Don't narrate what the reader is about to read ("let me walk you through…"). Don't test them. Assume their time is valuable — every sentence must earn its place.

### 3. Never talk down
Forbidden framings:
- "Most people get this wrong"
- "Nobody's talking about X"
- "The truth no one tells you"
- "You might be wondering…"
- "Let me break it down for you"
- "Actually…" as a correction to the reader
- Any "I know something you don't" energy

If consensus is incomplete, walk the reader through the reasoning. Let them arrive at the conclusion themselves. Don't announce their error.

### 4. Foster trust
Trust is earned through transparency, not claimed through confidence. Every thesis should:
- Cite specific data with units and timeframes (18% YoY, $500M AUM, 230bps, Q3 2025)
- Name at least one counterargument
- State what could invalidate the view
- Label inference vs. opinion vs. assumption when it matters
- Acknowledge uncertainty where it genuinely exists

"I've been wrong about this before" is a feature, not a weakness. Post-mortems build more trust than victory laps.

## Tone Markers

- **Precise on figures**: always cite units, timeframes, ranges. "Up meaningfully" is lazy; "up 18% YoY over six weeks" is useful.
- **Critical analysis built in**: every thesis names a counterargument and an invalidation condition. Not as hedge — as honesty.
- **Fact / inference / opinion / assumption distinguished** when it matters. "The data shows X. I infer Y. My opinion is Z. I'm assuming W."
- **Occasional warmth**: a rhetorical aside, a dry observation, a light self-deprecation. Not forced casualness; actual personality.
- **Ends with an invitation, not a test**: close with a question that invites perspective, not one that checks whether the reader followed along.

## Vocabulary

### Words we use
regime shift · capital allocation · basis points · pricing power · second-order effects · edge durability · hidden leverage · lagged repricing · hypothesis · provisional · weighted conclusion · material · ownable · compounding · convex · path-dependent · positioning · conviction · structural · mechanism

### Words we never use
synergy · delve · furthermore · "in today's fast-paced world" · "it goes without saying" · circle back · holistic · game-changer · game-changing · disruptive (as a buzzword) · unlock · leverage (as a verb) · ecosystem (unless literal) · any generic "AI changes everything" framing · any founder-motivation clichés

## Phrases

### Phrases we use
- "The non-obvious angle is…"
- "What could invalidate this view…"
- "Precision on facts, opinion in synthesis."
- "Here's what the data actually says."
- "I've been wrong about this before — here's how I think about it now."
- "The mechanism is…"
- "What I'm still uncertain about…"

### Phrases we don't use
- "You might be wondering…" (condescending)
- "Let me break it down for you." (patronizing)
- "Spoiler alert…"
- "This changes everything."
- "The one weird trick…"
- "Wake up." / "Hot take…"
- Anything that talks AT the reader instead of WITH them

## Formatting Rules

- Short sentences. One idea per paragraph. Line breaks between paragraphs.
- Numbers with specific units: 18% YoY, $500M AUM, 230bps, Q3 2025.
- No hashtag spam. LinkedIn: 3–5 max. X: rarely. Newsletter: none.
- Emoji: sparingly, and only where they genuinely reinforce meaning. Default is none.
- Lowercase for emphasis is fine on X occasionally; default is sentence case (Peter's register is slightly more formal than pure lowercase).
- Bullet points use `-`, not `•`.
- Bold (`**`) for the key claim in a paragraph; italics (`*`) for a hedged or provisional term.

## The 6 Brand Pillars

Every post should cluster into one or more of these. If it fits none, it's probably off-niche.

1. **Investor thinking** — risk framing, decision quality under uncertainty, capital allocation, incentives, edge durability.
2. **Founder reality** — building under uncertainty, product sequencing, customer pain, technical debt, hiring pressure, what the playbooks miss.
3. **Technical edge** — cloud systems, APIs, data pipelines, automation, quant workflows, system architecture.
4. **AI × finance** — AI-assisted research, signal generation, LLM workflows in trading, where AI helps, where it silently fails.
5. **Market intelligence** — what materially changed, why it matters now, what consensus is missing, narrative vs. fundamentals.
6. **Tools & frameworks** — checklists, teardowns, playbooks, mental models, research frameworks worth stealing.

## Anti-Patterns (Hard Bans)

Reject any draft that does one of these. Rewrite.

- **Smug contrarian takes** — "everyone thinks X, but they're wrong." Even if the contrarian case is correct, this framing talks down to the reader.
- **Teacher/student framing** — any implication that the reader doesn't know basic things. Assume competence.
- **Hot take style** — where provocation substitutes for insight. If the "take" isn't supported by specific evidence or a clear mechanism, it's not ready.
- **Clickbait superlatives** — "game-changing," "insane," "crazy," "must-read," "mind-blowing." All banned.
- **Lead with credentials** — credentials can appear (briefly) as supporting context, but the post opens with the insight, not the résumé.
- **Lecturing** — tell a story, walk through reasoning, or show a framework. Don't lecture.
- **Empty hype** — "AI is changing everything," "we're in a new era." Meaningless without a specific mechanism. Cut.
- **False certainty** — stating opinion as fact, or making predictions without probability ranges or invalidation conditions.

## The Respect Test

Before publishing (or before proposing a hook or draft to Peter), ask:

**"Would Peter be comfortable saying this out loud, verbatim, to a room of peers he respects?"**

If it sounds like lecturing, showboating, or talking down — even subtly — rewrite. This single test catches ~80% of voice violations.
