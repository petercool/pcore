# scheduling.md — Cadence, Peak Times, and Batch Workflow

> Posting cadence calibrated to Peter's 3 active platforms (v1). Revise quarterly based on engagement signals.

## v1 Weekly Cadence

| Platform | Posts/week | Recommended days |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| X | 7–10 | Daily (Mon–Fri primary, weekend lighter) |
| LinkedIn | 3 | Tue / Wed / Thu |
| Newsletter | 1 | Tue OR Thu |

**Rationale:**
- **X** rewards consistency and volume. 7–10/week hits the engagement floor without burnout.
- **LinkedIn** is optimized for weekdays — institutional audience reads during work hours. Mon is noisy, Fri is slow. Tue/Wed/Thu is prime.
- **Newsletter** once a week. Tuesday or Thursday mornings. Institutional audience reads over coffee, not on weekends.

## Peak Posting Times (US Eastern, institutional audience)

| Platform | Best times | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| X | 8–9am · 12–1pm · 5–6pm | Market-hours-adjacent bursts. 8–9am catches the pre-market read; 5–6pm catches the post-close reflection. |
| LinkedIn | 7–9am · 12pm · 5–6pm | 7–9am is strongest for this audience (morning inbox/feed check). |
| Newsletter | 8–10am Tue/Thu | Institutional audience reads newsletters in the first 2 hours of the workday. Not Monday (inbox overload). Not Friday (dropped into the weekend). |

## Batch Workflow

The content engine is designed for **batching**, not daily improvisation.

### Sunday (30–60 min) — Weekly Batch

1. Pick 2–3 topics for the week. Topics should:
   - Align with the niche (per `index.md`)
   - Cluster into 1–3 of Peter's 6 brand pillars
   - Each have at least one specific data point or mechanism to anchor on
2. Run the full repurposing chain for each topic (X → LinkedIn → Newsletter).
3. Output: 6–9 posts for the week (2–3 topics × 3 platforms).
4. Schedule posts using Buffer / Hypefury / Typefully / native scheduler. Stagger across the week — don't post all 3 platforms on the same topic on the same day.

### Monday–Friday (10–15 min/day) — Engagement

1. Check engagement on the previous day's posts.
2. Reply to meaningful comments in the first 60 minutes of posting (this window is where LinkedIn's algorithm decides post reach).
3. Note which hooks/angles drove engagement — rough mental tally, not a spreadsheet.

### Friday (20 min) — Weekly Review

1. What topics performed best? Note them for potential follow-ups or series.
2. What hook types drove engagement per platform? Add winners to `hooks.md`.
3. What fell flat? Diagnose: was the hook weak, the topic off-target, or the timing bad?
4. Any voice calibration to add? Update `platform-tone.md`.
5. Any audience signals (DM themes, comment patterns)? Update `builders.md` or `casual.md`.

## Staggering the Chain Across the Week

For a topic batched Sunday, a sensible stagger:

| Day | Platform | Why |
|-----|----------|-----|
| Tuesday 8am | X (thread) | Leads the week, strongest mid-week traffic |
| Wednesday 8am | LinkedIn post | One day later — different angle, doesn't feel like spam |
| Thursday 9am | Newsletter | Deepest version, arrives after readers have seen the shorter takes |

For a topic batched Sunday but with a reaction element (news-tied), the chain may collapse to Monday–Tuesday.

## Load Balancing — Avoid Topic Fatigue

- **Max 1 topic per week** goes through the full chain. A second topic may go X + LinkedIn (skip Newsletter). A third may be X-only.
- **Don't stack 3 posts on the same theme on the same platform in the same week.** Even if each is differently angled, the audience feels the repetition.
- **One post-mortem per month max.** Honest Correction hooks are powerful but lose impact if frequent.

## Breaking-News Exception

If a material event happens (rate decision, earnings surprise, geopolitical shock):
- **X** ships within an hour if Peter has a clear read. Don't wait for the full chain.
- **LinkedIn** ships within 24 hours with the framework/walk-through version.
- **Newsletter** ships the following Tuesday with the full reasoning + invalidation conditions.

Breaking-news posts should cite the specific event and timestamp in the first two lines — helps the algorithm and the reader.

## Iteration

Cadence is a hypothesis. Revise quarterly:
- If X performance stalls at 7/week, test 10/week for 4 weeks and compare engagement per post.
- If LinkedIn Tue/Wed/Thu underperforms, test Mon/Tue/Wed or Wed/Thu/Fri.
- If Newsletter open rate drops below 40%, revisit subject-line strategy and send-time.

Track only what changes Peter's decisions. Don't track for its own sake.
