# linkedin.md — LinkedIn Playbook

## Platform DNA

**"Strategic analysis delivered by a real person."**

LinkedIn is where investor thinking, founder reality, and institutional vocabulary land hardest. The audience expects rigor; they also reward warmth and genuine personality. Board-ready but not corporate.

## Content Rules

- **Length:** 1,300–2,000 characters is the sweet spot. Max 3,000. Under 800 usually reads as thin.
- **Fold:** ~210 characters before "see more" truncation. The first two lines must earn the click.
- **Paragraphs:** 1–2 sentences each. Line breaks between paragraphs. Dense walls of text kill reach.
- **One core argument** per post. Supporting points can be 2–3, but the thesis is one.
- **End with a question** that invites perspective, not one that tests the reader.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 maximum, at the end, meaningful (e.g., #AIinFinance, #PortfolioConstruction, #QuantResearch). No hashtag spam.
- **Links:** LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes posts with external links. If linking is necessary, put the link in the first comment, not the post body.
- **Tag only with purpose.** Tagging someone should serve the post, not chase attention.

## Voice Calibration

Per `voice/platform-tone.md` → LinkedIn section:
- Professional and warm
- Institutional vocabulary welcome (native to this audience)
- Narrative arcs welcome
- Personality through dry observation, rhetorical aside, genuine curiosity
- Never "excited to share…" openers
- Never lecturing. Walk-through, not teach-out.

## Five Formats That Work

### 1. Analytical Teardown
Deep decomposition of a single event, dataset, or thesis. Numbers throughout. One counterargument inline.

**Hook options:** Data Lead, Non-Obvious Angle
**Example topic:** "Decomposing NVDA's 32% Q3 beat — what's demand, what's mix, what's pull-forward"

### 2. Non-Obvious Angle Thesis
A specific read the consensus is missing. Framed as addition to the conversation, not correction.

**Hook options:** Non-Obvious Angle, Collision
**Example topic:** "The non-obvious angle on AI capex isn't demand — it's the unit economics of power at the marginal cluster"

### 3. Framework Walk-Through
A reusable decision or analysis framework, walked through with a concrete example.

**Hook options:** Framework, Walk-Through
**Example topic:** "The 4-step framework I use for sizing a speculative position under uncertainty"

### 4. Market Observation
A specific, recent observation + what it signals. Shorter than a teardown, more precise than a hot take.

**Hook options:** Data Lead, Collision
**Example topic:** "US 10Y real yields hit 2.3% this week. The portfolio-construction assumption this breaks:"

### 5. Founder-Operator Lesson
A real lesson from building or running, including what didn't work. Honest, specific, useful.

**Hook options:** Honest Correction, Walk-Through, Proof
**Example topic:** "We shipped the wrong execution architecture last year. Here's what I'd do differently now."

## Post Structure Template

```
[Hook — 1–2 lines, ≤210 chars, per hooks.md]

[Thesis — 1 paragraph, bolded or emphasized]

[Support point 1 — short paragraph, at least one specific number]

[Support point 2 — short paragraph]

[Counterargument / invalidation — 1 short paragraph]

[Closing — a genuine question inviting perspective]

[Hashtags — 3–5 max, end of post]
```

## Posting Strategy

- **Cadence:** 3 posts/week (see `engine/scheduling.md`)
- **Days:** Tue / Wed / Thu (Mon noisy, Fri weak)
- **Times:** 7–9am Eastern primary; 12pm and 5–6pm secondary
- **Timing tactics:**
  - Post, then reply to comments in the first 60 minutes. This window determines reach.
  - If a post underperforms at 8am, don't delete — LinkedIn sometimes picks posts up on day 2.

## Audience

- **Primary:** builders (per `audience/builders.md`)
- **Secondary:** casual (per `audience/casual.md`)
- **Balance:** default builder-primary with casual-accessible framing. Roughly every 4–5 posts, explicitly frame for the casual audience (strategic read, lighter jargon).

## Repurposing Notes

LinkedIn should differ from X on the same topic in these ways:

| Dimension | X | LinkedIn |
|-----------|---|----------|
| Angle | Sharpest single facet | Framework, walk-through, or different facet |
| Hook | Data Lead / Collision / Non-Obvious Angle | Framework / Walk-Through / Non-Obvious Angle |
| Length | 280 chars or 5–12 tweet thread | 1,300–2,000 chars |
| Tone | Direct, punchy, light humor allowed | Professional, warm, institutional |
| Depth | Surface insight | Walk-through of reasoning + counterargument |
| Close | Concrete takeaway or question | Question inviting perspective |

If the LinkedIn post reads like a reformatted X thread, rewrite with a different angle.

## Things to Avoid on LinkedIn

- "Excited to share…" / "Thrilled to announce…" — weak, generic
- Humble-brags dressed as lessons
- Screenshotting a tweet and calling it a LinkedIn post
- Hashtag spam (#leadership #entrepreneurship #growth)
- Vague "X lessons from my journey" posts without specific content
- Linking out in the post body (kills reach; put links in comments)
- Opening with credentials — lead with the insight

## LinkedIn-Specific Opportunities

- **Document posts** (PDF carousels) — high reach, good for Framework and Analytical Teardown formats. Consider for Phase 2.
- **LinkedIn articles** — for Long-Form Text that doesn't warrant a full Newsletter. Useful when the content is LinkedIn-native rather than an audience-building piece.
- **Polls** — use sparingly. Good for gauging a specific strategic question; bad as a lazy substitute for a real post.
