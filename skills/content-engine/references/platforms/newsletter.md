# newsletter.md — Newsletter Playbook

## Platform DNA

**"The deepest, most personal take Peter shares — reflective, honest, warm."**

The Newsletter is where the full reasoning lives. Readers opted in for depth. This is also where Peter can show intellectual honesty in ways the shorter platforms can't carry — post-mortems, full invalidation conditions, "what I'm still working through" notes. These build the trust that compounds the brand.

## Content Rules

- **Length:** 1,000–2,000 words sweet spot. 800 if the idea is tight; up to 2,500 only if the content genuinely requires it.
- **Frequency:** 1 issue per week. Tuesday or Thursday morning (see `engine/scheduling.md`).
- **Subject line:** under 55 characters. Curiosity, Data Lead, or Walk-Through framing (per `engine/hooks.md`). No clickbait; the subject should promise what the issue delivers.
- **Preview text (first 80–100 chars):** extends the subject line. Second hook. Don't waste it on "read this issue in your browser."
- **Structure:** opening + thesis + 2–5 supporting sections + counterargument/invalidation + close. Section headers welcome.
- **Reading time note (optional):** if the issue is 1,500+ words, a "~7 min read" line at the top respects the reader's time.
- **Visuals:** charts, tables, or diagrams where they add — not as decoration.
- **Footnotes:** welcome for sources, sidebars, or pedantic caveats that would interrupt flow inline.
- **Close with a genuine note or a reply-inviting question.** Newsletters that invite direct reply build the highest-quality audience relationships.

## Voice Calibration

Per `voice/platform-tone.md` → Newsletter section:
- Most personal. "Writing to a thoughtful friend who works in adjacent domains."
- Reflective, curious, allowed to be uncertain.
- "I'm still working through…" is native here.
- Include "what I got wrong last time" and "what could invalidate this view" sections where relevant.
- Section headers should advance the argument, not just label it.

## Four Formats That Work

### 1. Weekly Brief
Synthesis of what materially changed in the past week + Peter's read on implications.

**Structure:**
- Opening: one specific recent event or data point
- What's new (2–3 sections, each with one supporting data point)
- Peter's read: weighted view with probability ranges
- What could invalidate
- Close: question or personal note

**Hook options:** Data Lead, Collision

### 2. Deep Dive
Full decomposition of one topic. The longest format. 1,500–2,500 words.

**Structure:**
- Opening: hook + why this topic now + scope ("what I'll cover; what I'm leaving out")
- Setup: necessary context (minimal — the audience is capable)
- Main sections (2–4): each a distinct facet with data and mechanism
- Synthesis: the weighted view
- Counterargument: the strongest case against
- Invalidation: specific conditions under which Peter would change his mind
- Close: "what I'm still uncertain about" + question

**Hook options:** Walk-Through, Collision, Non-Obvious Angle

### 3. Framework Guide
A reusable framework taught through a specific example, with enough depth for the reader to apply it.

**Structure:**
- Opening: the decision or problem the framework addresses
- The framework (N steps, with why each step matters)
- Worked example (one real case, with numbers)
- Where the framework breaks (honest limitations)
- Close: invitation to share their version

**Hook options:** Framework, Walk-Through

### 4. Post-Mortem (signature format)
Honest reflection on something Peter got wrong — a trade, a forecast, a decision, a build. Signature content for the trust dimension.

**Structure:**
- Opening: "Here's what I got wrong, specifically."
- The call / decision at the time (with the reasoning, fairly presented)
- What happened
- What I missed (specific mechanism or signal)
- What I'd change
- What this updates in my model going forward
- Close: invitation for readers who've seen similar patterns

**Hook options:** Honest Correction
**Cadence:** 1 per quarter maximum. Loses power if frequent.

## Posting Strategy

- **Cadence:** 1 issue per week
- **Day:** Tuesday OR Thursday (pick one; consistency matters more than which)
- **Time:** 8–10am Eastern. Institutional readers check inboxes in the first 2 hours of the workday.
- **Avoid:** Monday (inbox overload), Friday (weekend drop-off), weekends.

## Audience

- **Primary:** builders + casual (Newsletter is Peter's most audience-balanced platform — both groups subscribe for depth)
- **Engagement signal:** replies. Newsletter replies are Peter's highest-signal audience input. Treat them accordingly.

## Repurposing Notes

The Newsletter goes **third** in the chain per `engine/repurpose.md`, after X and LinkedIn. Reasoning:
- X forces the core.
- LinkedIn adds a different angle with institutional framing.
- Newsletter goes deep — full mechanism, full invalidation, post-mortem elements.

Newsletter on a topic should differ from LinkedIn on the same topic by:
- Length (Newsletter is 3–5x longer)
- Depth of mechanism (Newsletter walks through the full chain of reasoning)
- Inclusion of invalidation and post-mortem elements (usually absent from LinkedIn)
- Personal texture (more reflective, more allowed to be uncertain)

Never copy the LinkedIn post into the Newsletter and call it done. The Newsletter has room for the full reasoning — use it.

## Things to Avoid in the Newsletter

- Padding for length. If it's 900 words, publish 900.
- Hedging into meaninglessness. Have a view. Back it. Name invalidation.
- Subject lines that over-promise ("The AI thesis is dead" — unless you can defend that exact claim)
- Subject lines that under-promise ("This week's update") — weak, forgettable
- Opening with "Hope you had a great week" boilerplate. Respect the reader's time.
- Recycled LinkedIn content. The subscribers opted in for something more.
- Corporate "issue X of Y" framing. Editions are fine; numbering isn't load-bearing.

## Newsletter-Specific Opportunities

- **Post-mortem format** is signature and differentiating. 1 per quarter. High-trust content.
- **Reader-reply framing** — close with a question that makes replying easy and valuable. Builds the highest-quality relationships in Peter's audience.
- **Guest angles** — occasionally include a specific case from someone in the audience (with permission). Builds community; makes the newsletter less one-directional.
- **Series arcs** — 3–5 issues exploring a single large theme (e.g., "AI capex cycle, Part 1 of 4"). Series earn higher sustained engagement than one-off issues.
