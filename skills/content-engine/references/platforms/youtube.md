## To fill when activating:

- **Platform DNA** — Most structured and educational. Authority voice — you're the expert teaching a class — but still peer energy, never lecturing. Replication-focused: the viewer should be able to reproduce what's demonstrated.
- **Content Rules** — 8–12 min sweet spot for tutorials; 15–20 min for deep dives. SEO-optimized titles, descriptions, chapters, timestamps.
- **Voice Calibration** — More structured than other platforms. Longer sentences allowed. Authority voice without condescension — still peer-to-peer (imagine a senior engineer presenting to other senior engineers, not to juniors).
- **Formats** — Tutorial walk-through (primary), System architecture explainer, Post-mortem video (rare but high-value), Case study breakdown.
- **Posting Strategy** — 1–2x/week, Tue or Thu upload (indexes overnight), peak traffic 2–4pm.
- **Audience** — Builders-heavy on YouTube. The technical audience watches tutorials; the casual audience rarely does.
- **Repurposing Notes** — YouTube goes at Step 6 of the chain per `engine/repurpose.md`. Only topics that genuinely benefit from visual/video walk-through warrant a YouTube version.
