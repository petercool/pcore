# Platform Style Reference

## LinkedIn
- **Tone:** Professional but human. Authoritative yet accessible. "Strategic risk memo in public form."
- **Hook:** Max ~210 characters before "see more" fold. Must earn the click.
- **Structure:** Short paragraphs (1–2 sentences). Line breaks between paragraphs. End with a question or CTA.
- **Length:** 150–300 words optimal. Up to 3,000 characters.
- **Best hooks:** Credibility, Value, Eloquence, Counter-narrative.
- **Best post types:** Analytical, Contrarian, Observation, Actionable.
- **Avoid:** Hashtag spam (max 3–5), overly casual/meme language, clickbait words ("massive", "crazy", "must-watch", "game changer").
- **Board/institutional vocabulary:** regime shift, capital allocation, risk governance, pricing power, balance-sheet resilience, policy flexibility, second-order effects, portfolio construction, earnings durability, hidden leverage, lagged repricing.

## X / Twitter
- **Tone:** Direct, punchy, opinionated. Personality-forward.
- **Structure:** Hook in first line. Threads for longer content (each tweet stands alone). One idea per tweet.
- **Length:** Under 280 characters per tweet. Threads: 3–15 tweets.
- **Best hooks:** Curiosity, Surprise, Counter-narrative, Counter-intuitive.
- **Best post types:** Contrarian, X vs Y, Listicle, Actionable.
- **Avoid:** Verbose phrasing. Corporate speak. Walls of text.

## Instagram (Captions)
- **Tone:** Authentic, conversational, visually-aware. Copy supports the image.
- **Structure:** Hook in first line (~125 chars before truncation). Hashtags at bottom (15–20).
- **Length:** 150–2,200 characters. Optimal: 150–300.
- **Best hooks:** Faces, Inspiration, Celebration, Identity.
- **Best post types:** Motivation, Observation, Listicle.

## Blog / Long-form
- **Tone:** Institutional but readable. Data-dense with narrative flow.
- **Structure:** Opening hook (2-sentence framing), one thesis per section, bold inline callouts for key metrics, image/chart placeholders after thesis paragraphs.
- **Length:** 800–2,500 words.
- **Reference style:** Callum Thomas' ChartStorm — tight openers, direct tone, charts reinforcing arguments.

## Podcast Script
- **Tone:** Professional, calm, buy-side morning meeting cadence.
- **Structure:** McKinsey elevator principle — 10s conclusion → 30s three points → 1min why it matters → 3min full briefing.
- **Length:** 600–1,000 words (~3 minutes).
- **Rules:** Short sentences, explain jargon in plain language, numbers oral-friendly.

## Email Newsletter
- **Tone:** Personal, direct, conversational. As if writing to one person.
- **Subject line:** Under 50 characters. Curiosity, Fear, Surprise, or Value hooks.
- **Structure:** Short paragraphs. One CTA per email. P.S. lines for secondary hooks.
- **Length:** 100–300 words for outreach. Newsletters can be longer.
