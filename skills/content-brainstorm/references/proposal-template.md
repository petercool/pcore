## Worked Example

Source material assumed: a quarterly macro briefing noting (a) NVDA Q3 revenue beat consensus by 32%, (b) Brent crude up 18% on Middle East tension, (c) Fed signaling pause despite sticky core PCE at 3.4%.

### Angle 1: Can the AI Capex Cycle Survive a $100 Oil Print?

**Lens:** Lens 4: Cross-Domain Collision
**Hook type:** Hook 4: Curiosity
**Post type:** Post type 6: Analytical
**Platform fit:** LinkedIn (primary), blog (secondary as long-form expansion)
**Engagement potential:** High — pairs two of the most-watched narratives of the quarter; institutional audience has not seen them framed against each other.

#### Headline
The AI Trade and the Oil Trade Are on a Collision Course. Here's What Breaks First.

#### Hook (first 1–3 lines, before "see more" fold)
NVDA just beat by 32%. Brent is up 18% in six weeks. The market is treating these as separate stories.
They're not. One of them is about to constrain the other — and the constraint isn't priced.

#### Description / Body Direction
Body walks through the mechanism: hyperscaler capex commitments are denominated in dollars, but the marginal energy cost of training compute is rising with crude (data center power is ~60% gas-linked in the US, ~80% globally). At $100 Brent sustained, the implied operating cost on a 100MW training cluster rises roughly 11–14%, compressing the unit economics that justify the capex spend in the first place. Conclusion: the AI capex thesis is more rate-sensitive *and* more energy-sensitive than consensus models reflect — a 6-month look-through, not a same-day repricing. CTA: "Which two narratives in your portfolio are quietly correlated?"

#### Image / Chart Plan
- **Type:** Generate
- **Concept:** Divergence Chart — two lines on the same axis: hyperscaler capex run-rate (LHS, $B annualized) and Brent crude (RHS, $/bbl), 2022–present. Annotate the point where the gap starts to matter for unit economics.
- **Why it works:** The visual *is* the thesis — two lines that look unrelated turn out to share a structural floor. Reinforces the "collision course" framing of the headline.
- **Carousel (optional):** 3 slides — (1) Divergence Chart hook, (2) cost decomposition table for a 100MW cluster at $70 vs $100 Brent, (3) summary card "What this means for the AI trade."

## Notes on the Example

- **Lens citation drives the angle's reason for existing** — without "Cross-Domain Collision" as the lens, this would have been written as a generic AI piece or a generic oil piece. The lens is what makes it differentiated.
- **Hook type drives wording** — Curiosity hook = open loop ("here's what breaks first") that forces the reader to keep going.
- **Post type drives structure** — Analytical post = data-walkthrough body, not a one-liner take.
- **The numbers in the hook are specific** (32%, 18%, six weeks) — vague hooks ("AI is booming, oil is rising") would fail the Quality Checklist.
- **The image is the thesis, not decoration** — the divergence chart literally shows the collision the headline promises.

When generating proposals in Step 3, every angle should reach this level of specificity. If an angle can't be filled out to this depth, it's probably weak and should be cut.
