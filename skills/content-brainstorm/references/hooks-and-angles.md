# Hooks, Post Types, and Angle Strategies

## 12 Hook Types

| # | Hook | Trigger Reaction | Example |
|---|------|-----------------|---------|
| 1 | **Credibility** | "This person knows what they're talking about." | "After 10 years managing $500M..." |
| 2 | **Fear** | "I hope I'm not doing it wrong." | "3 portfolio mistakes that silently destroy returns" |
| 3 | **Inspiration** | "How can I get that?" | "She went from 0 to $1M ARR in 11 months" |
| 4 | **Curiosity** | "Then what?" | "The one metric Wall Street doesn't want you to track" |
| 5 | **Counter-narrative** | "Wait, that's the opposite of what I thought!" | "Diversification is destroying your returns" |
| 6 | **Counter-intuitive** | "I never thought of it that way." | "Raising prices increased our customer satisfaction" |
| 7 | **Eloquence** | "Finally, someone said it." | "The market doesn't care about your thesis" |
| 8 | **Faces** | Visual hook — pair with authentic photo | Best on visual platforms |
| 9 | **Value** | "I need to save this." | "7 valuation shortcuts every analyst should know" |
| 10 | **Surprise** | "Oh, that's cool." | "Warren Buffett made 99% of his wealth after age 50" |
| 11 | **Celebration** | "I'm so happy for you." | "We just closed our Series A" |
| 12 | **Identity** | "Hey, that's me." | "Solo founders: this one's for you" |

## 8 Post Types

1. **Actionable** — Step-by-step how-to. "Here's how to [achieve X] in [N] steps."
2. **Observation** — What you've noticed. "I've noticed [observation]. Here's why it matters."
3. **X vs Y** — Comparison with insight. "[Thing A] vs [Thing B]. The difference? [Insight]."
4. **Present / Future** — Today vs. prediction. "Today, [state]. In 5 years, [prediction]."
5. **Contrarian** — Spicy take. "Unpopular opinion: [bold claim]."
6. **Analytical** — Data teardown. "I broke down [subject]. Here's what I found."
7. **Motivation** — Inspire action. "[Inspiring statement]. You can do this too."
8. **Listicle** — Curated list. "[N] [things] every [audience] should know."

## Angle Generation Strategies

When brainstorming angles from source material, systematically apply these lenses:

### Lens 1: Audience Perspective Rotation
Ask: "Who cares about this, and why?" Generate one angle per audience segment:
- Portfolio managers / allocators
- Risk committees / boards
- Retail investors
- Sector specialists (tech, energy, credit, etc.)
- Adjacent professionals (consultants, founders, operators)

### Lens 2: Timeframe Rotation
Same data, different horizons:
- **Today** — What's actionable right now?
- **This week** — What should I watch?
- **This quarter** — What's the positioning thesis?
- **This cycle** — What's the regime change?

### Lens 3: Contrarian Inversion
For every consensus view, ask: "What if the opposite is true?"
- Consensus: "Energy trade is the play" → Contrarian: "Energy trade is crowded — the marginal alpha is elsewhere"
- Consensus: "AI is a bubble" → Contrarian: "MU's 196% YoY beat says the supercycle is real"

### Lens 4: Cross-Domain Collision
Find where two narratives collide:
- AI earnings + oil shock = "Can $1T NVDA revenue survive $100 oil?"
- Private credit stress + retail income ETFs = "Hidden leverage most investors don't know they own"
- Fed policy trap + stagflation = "What do you own when the Fed can't move?"

### Lens 5: Data Surprise Extraction
Scan for the most surprising numbers and build hooks around them:
- Revenue beat by 100%+ → Surprise hook
- Index down 20% that nobody's discussing → Fear + Curiosity
- Probability framework (55/30/15) → Value hook (shareable mental model)

### Lens 6: Emotional Resonance
Map the data to human concerns:
- Fear: "Your portfolio isn't positioned for this"
- Relief: "Here's the scenario where everything normalizes"
- Vindication: "If you called the energy trade early, here's what's next"
- Anxiety: "The market assumes this won't get out of control"
